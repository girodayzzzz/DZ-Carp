Core Rigs Every Carp Angler Should Know

Hair Rig

The classic carp rig for boilies, maize and pellets. Keep the hair length matched to your hookbait size for better hook holds.

Blowback Rig

Ideal when fish are cautious. The ring on the shank allows the bait to move naturally and helps the hook turn faster.

Spinner Rig

How to Build Better Rigs

Choose the Right Hook Pattern

Curve shank and wide gape hooks are versatile options. Match the hook size to bait size and fish pressure.

Balance Your Hookbait

Critically balanced baits react quickly when carp feed. Add putty to keep the bait sitting naturally on the lake bed.

Test Before Casting

Always test your rig in the edge. Check that the hook turns, the bait behaves naturally and the lead system ejects safely.

Rig Selection by Situation

Clean Gravel Spots

Use a simple hair rig with a bottom bait or wafter for a clean and direct presentation.

Light Weed

Use a chod-style or pop-up presentation to keep the hookbait visible just above debris.

Heavy Angling Pressure

Try a subtle blowback or longer rig with smaller hooks and balanced baits to fool cautious carp.
